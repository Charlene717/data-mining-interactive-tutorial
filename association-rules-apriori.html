故事開場

場景：Walmart 的星期五夜

SCENE · WALMART 1992

啤酒、尿布、與一篇 Wall Street Journal 的傳奇

1992 年某天，Teradata 的 Thomas Blischok 與他的團隊在分析 Walmart 25 家分店的 120 萬筆交易資料。他們跑了當時還沒有名字的「關聯規則探勘」演算法，並在星期五傍晚的時段（下午 5–7 點）發現了一個奇特的模式：購買尿布的男性顧客，有顯著的機率也會買啤酒。

1993 年 12 月 23 日，Wall Street Journal 的 Pamela Brown 寫了一篇報導，這個故事從此在資料探勘教科書裡流傳——啤酒與尿布、Walmart、男人下班順道幫太太買嬰兒用品、自己也順手帶 6 罐啤酒回家。完美的故事。

真相？學界後來考證：(1) 確實有相關性，但效應遠小於傳說；(2) Walmart 從未根據此調整商品陳列；(3) 真正讓零售業擁抱關聯規則的是 1994 年 Agrawal-Srikant 在 VLDB 發表的 Apriori 演算法。但故事的力量讓「資料探勘 = 找意外的規則」這個概念深植人心——這就是本章要教你的技藝。

One day in 1992, Thomas Blischok at Teradata and his team were mining 1.2M transactions from 25 Walmart stores. They ran what was then called nameless "association mining" and noticed something strange in the Friday-evening slot (5–7 p.m.): men buying diapers had a much higher chance of also buying beer.

On 23 December 1993, Pamela Brown wrote it up in the Wall Street Journal, and the story has been in data-mining textbooks ever since — beer and diapers, Walmart, dads grabbing baby supplies on the way home and tossing a six-pack in for themselves. The perfect anecdote.

The truth? Academia later established: (1) a correlation did exist, but the effect was weaker than legend; (2) Walmart never actually rearranged shelves based on it; (3) what really pushed retail to adopt association mining was Agrawal-Srikant's 1994 VLDB Apriori paper. But the story is so powerful that "data mining = finding surprising rules" lodged in every business person's head — and that's exactly the craft this chapter teaches.

核心詞彙

12 個關鍵術語

Transaction
交易
一次購物的「購物車」——裡面有什麼項目都記下來。
One shopping cart — record what items it contains.
T_i ⊆ I，I 是全部 item 的集合。生醫例：一張處方單、一位病人 ICD 碼集合、一次手術的事件項目。
T_i ⊆ I, where I is the set of all items. Bio examples: a prescription, a patient's ICD codes, the events in one surgery.
Itemset
項集
幾個東西一起出現的小組合，如 {啤酒, 尿布}。
A small combo of items that show up together, like {beer, diapers}.
X ⊆ I 的任意子集；k-itemset 含 k 個元素。空 itemset 與全集分別 trivially 真。
Any subset X ⊆ I; a k-itemset contains k elements. Empty and full sets are trivially supported.
Support
支援度
這個組合出現幾次？除以總交易數，就是支援度。
How often does this combo show up? Divide by total transactions = support.
supp(X) = |{T : X ⊆ T}| / |D|；min_support 設太低 → 規則爆炸；太高 → 漏掉罕見但有意義的規則。
supp(X) = |{T : X ⊆ T}| / |D|; min_support too low → rule explosion, too high → miss rare-but-meaningful rules.
Confidence
信賴度
買 X 的人裡有多少也買 Y？
Of the people who buy X, how many also buy Y?
conf(X⇒Y) = P(Y|X) = supp(X∪Y)/supp(X)。經典陷阱：Y 本來就很常見時 conf 自然高，要用 lift 矯正。
conf(X⇒Y) = P(Y|X) = supp(X∪Y)/supp(X). Classic trap: if Y is already common, conf is high by default — use lift to correct.
Lift
提升度
X 出現時 Y 的機率，比 Y 本來的機率高多少倍。
How much more likely Y is when X appears, compared to Y's baseline.
lift = P(Y|X)/P(Y) = conf/supp(Y)。>1 正相關、=1 獨立、<1 負相關。對稱：lift(X⇒Y) = lift(Y⇒X)。
lift = P(Y|X)/P(Y) = conf/supp(Y). >1 positive, =1 independent, <1 negative. Symmetric: lift(X⇒Y) = lift(Y⇒X).
Leverage
槓桿值
實際出現次數 − 假如獨立應該出現的次數——差多少？
Actual co-occurrence minus what it would be if independent — the gap.
leverage = P(X∪Y) − P(X)P(Y)。Piatetsky-Shapiro 1991。不像 lift 對「rare item」過敏，更穩定但較少用。
leverage = P(X∪Y) − P(X)P(Y). Piatetsky-Shapiro 1991. Unlike lift, not skewed by rare items — more stable, less popular.
Conviction
堅信度
X 多麼「斷言」Y 一定要出現？無窮大表示完全暗示。
How strongly does X "insist" Y must appear? Infinity = X always implies Y.
conv = (1−supp(Y))/(1−conf)。1 = 獨立，∞ = X always implies Y。對 confidence=1 很敏感。
conv = (1−supp(Y))/(1−conf). 1 = independent, ∞ = X always implies Y. Sensitive to confidence=1.
Apriori principle
Apriori 原理（反單調性）
如果一小組不夠常見，加更多東西進來只會更不常見——可以跳過不算。
If a small set isn't frequent, adding more items only makes it rarer — you can skip them.
downward closure / anti-monotone：supp(X) < min_support ⇒ ∀Y ⊇ X, supp(Y) < min_support。這就是 Apriori 演算法剪枝的關鍵。
Downward closure / anti-monotone: supp(X) < min_support ⇒ ∀Y ⊇ X, supp(Y) < min_support. The key pruning rule of Apriori.
FP-tree / FP-Growth
FP-tree / FP-Growth
把交易資料壓成一棵樹，重複的部分共用節點——只要掃資料兩次就能找頻繁項。
Compress the transactions into a tree where shared parts share nodes — just two DB scans to find frequent itemsets.
Han-Pei-Yin 2000 (SIGMOD)；建構 prefix-tree（FP-tree），項目按頻率排序，分支共用前綴。透過遞迴 conditional FP-tree 找頻繁項集，無候選產生。
Han-Pei-Yin 2000 (SIGMOD); builds a prefix-tree (FP-tree) with items sorted by frequency, sharing prefixes. Recursive conditional FP-trees yield frequent itemsets without candidate generation.
Eclat
Eclat
每個 item 記它出現過哪幾筆交易（TID list），看兩個 TID 清單的交集就知道共現次數。
For each item, list which transactions it appears in (TID list); intersect lists to get co-occurrence count.
Zaki 2000；垂直表示法，supp(X∪Y) = |tidset(X) ∩ tidset(Y)|。記憶體效率高，適合 mid-size sparse data。
Zaki 2000; vertical representation, supp(X∪Y) = |tidset(X) ∩ tidset(Y)|. Memory-efficient, good for mid-size sparse data.
Closed itemset
閉合項集
沒有更長的「同樣常見」的超集——是該支援度的代表。
No longer super-set with the same support — the "representative" at that support level.
X closed iff ∀Y ⊃ X, supp(Y) < supp(X)。Maximal itemset 更嚴格：X maximal iff ∀Y ⊃ X, Y 不頻繁。closed ⊇ maximal。
X closed iff ∀Y ⊃ X, supp(Y) < supp(X). Maximal is stricter: X maximal iff ∀Y ⊃ X, Y infrequent. closed ⊇ maximal.

Use Gene Ontology as side info to help association rules "understand biology".

比喻

一句話講清楚關聯規則

🎧
SPOTIFY ANALOGY

關聯規則就是 Spotify 的「聽 A 的人也聽 B」

關聯規則做的事，跟 Spotify、Netflix、Amazon 的「相關推薦」很像——只不過是用「購物車一起出現」、「同一張處方一起開」、「同一患者同時帶 ICD 碼」當作「聽過」。
三大指標可以這樣理解：support 是「歌曲熱門度」（你不會推薦沒人聽過的歌）、confidence 是「聽 A 的人裡有多少也聽 B」、lift 是「B 真的因為 A 才更可能被聽，還是 B 本來就是排行榜冠軍」。

Association rules do what Spotify/Netflix/Amazon's "related" features do — except "co-occurrence in a cart", "co-prescription", or "co-ICD-code in one patient" replaces "listened to".
The three metrics map cleanly: support is "song popularity" (you don't recommend a song nobody's heard), confidence is "of A-listeners, how many also like B", lift is "does B truly get a boost from A, or is B just always #1 anyway".

點開看：Lift = 因果關係嗎？
不是。Lift 只測「統計相關」，不測「因果」。經典反例：{雨傘} ⇒ {下雨} lift 很高，但帶雨傘不會「導致」下雨。生醫上更危險：{某藥} ⇒ {某副作用} 高 lift 可能是 (1) 因果、(2) confounding（兩者都被疾病嚴重度引起）、(3) 反向因果（副作用先出現所以才開藥）、(4) Berkson's bias（只看住院病人）。對策：將 lift 作為「值得進一步研究的訊號」，而非結論——後續配合 RCT 或 Mendelian Randomization。
No. Lift measures statistical association, not causation. Classic counter-example: {umbrella} ⇒ {rain} has high lift, but carrying an umbrella doesn't cause rain. In biomedicine it's worse: {drug} ⇒ {adverse event} with high lift could be (1) causal, (2) confounding (both caused by disease severity), (3) reverse causation (adverse event preceded the prescription), (4) Berkson's bias (only hospitalized patients). Fix: treat lift as "signal worth investigating", not a conclusion — follow up with RCT or Mendelian Randomization.

概覽

交易、項集、規則

輸入資料是「交易資料庫」：每筆交易 T 是一個項目集合（如購物車、共現症狀、共處方）。任意項目組合稱為 itemset，被夠多交易支持的稱為 frequent itemset。形如 X ⇒ Y 的稱為 關聯規則（X、Y 互不相交）。

生物醫學應用：共現症狀網絡、共處方藥物模式、SNP 共現、ICD 碼共現、單細胞共表達。Nature Sci Rep 2026 提出的 ASEA（Association Subgraph Enrichment Analysis）就是把關聯規則與基因本體 (Gene Ontology) 結合的 ontology-driven 變體。

The input is a transaction database: each transaction T is a set of items (shopping cart, co-occurring symptoms, co-prescriptions). Any item combination is an itemset; if enough transactions support it, it's frequent. An association rule takes the form X ⇒ Y (X and Y disjoint).

Biomedical uses: symptom co-occurrence networks, co-prescription patterns, SNP co-occurrence, ICD code combinations, single-cell co-expression. ASEA (Association Subgraph Enrichment Analysis, Nature Sci Rep 2026) is an ontology-driven variant that fuses association rules with the Gene Ontology.

三大指標

一、Support / Confidence / Lift

指標公式意義
SupportP(X∪Y) = |T含X∪Y| / |T|該組合出現的頻率，過低代表罕見Frequency of the combination — low = rare
ConfidenceP(Y|X) = supp(X∪Y) / supp(X)在含 X 的交易中，含 Y 的比例Among X-transactions, fraction also containing Y
LiftP(Y|X) / P(Y) = conf / supp(Y)>1 正相關、=1 獨立、<1 負相關>1 positive, =1 independent, <1 negative
LeverageP(X∪Y) − P(X)·P(Y)超出獨立假設的「絕對」差Absolute deviation from independence
Conviction(1−supp(Y)) / (1−conf)∞ 表示 X 完全暗示 Y；1 表示獨立∞ if X always implies Y; 1 if independent
💡
為什麼需要 Lift？高信賴度可能只是因為 Y 本來就常見（如「啤酒 ⇒ 麵包」conf=0.9，但其實買麵包的本來就 0.92）。Lift > 1 才代表 X 真的「提升」了 Y 出現的可能性。 Why lift? High confidence can simply reflect Y being common (e.g., beer ⇒ bread with 0.9 confidence, but bread itself is bought by 92%). Lift > 1 means X genuinely raises the chance of Y.
演算法

二、三大演算法

Apriori (1994)

逐層搜尋：先找 1-itemset，再合併成候選 2-itemset，掃資料庫過濾，再 3-itemset…。核心利器是 反單調性 (anti-monotone)：若任一子集不頻繁，超集必不頻繁——可大幅剪枝。

Level-wise search: find 1-itemsets, join into candidate 2-itemsets, scan DB to prune, then 3-itemsets... Key: the anti-monotone property — if a subset is infrequent, every superset must be — enabling massive pruning.

FP-Growth (2000)

建立壓縮的 FP-tree，僅需兩次掃資料庫，避免 Apriori 的候選集爆炸。在密集資料上比 Apriori 快 10–100×。

Builds a compact FP-tree; only two DB scans, avoiding Apriori's candidate explosion. On dense data, 10–100× faster than Apriori.

Eclat (2000)

使用 垂直 tidlist（每個 item 記錄它出現在哪些交易 ID）。藉由 tidlist 交集計算支援度，記憶體效率高，適合中等大小資料。

Uses vertical tidlists (each item lists transaction IDs it appears in). Support is computed by tidlist intersection — memory-efficient, good for mid-sized data.

⚠️
規則數爆炸：低 support 可產生數百萬規則。實務上會 (1) 設定足夠的 min_support，(2) 取 Top-N by Lift，(3) 用 closed 或 maximal itemset 限縮輸出，(4) 視覺化用 arulesViz 的 grouped matrix / graph。 Rule explosion: low support yields millions of rules. In practice: (1) set min_support high enough, (2) take Top-N by lift, (3) restrict to closed or maximal itemsets, (4) visualize with arulesViz grouped matrix / graph.

L2 = 8 itemsets.
Step 4
產生 C3。從 L2 自連，保留 prefix 相同者：{麵包,牛奶,尿布}、{麵包,牛奶,啤酒}、{麵包,尿布,啤酒}、{牛奶,尿布,啤酒}、{牛奶,尿布,可樂}、{麵包,牛奶,可樂}。Apriori 剪枝：{麵包,牛奶,可樂} 含 {麵包,可樂}（不在 L2），刪除。共 5 個候選。 Generate C3. Self-join L2, keep same-prefix joins: {bread,milk,diapers}, {bread,milk,beer}, {bread,diapers,beer}, {milk,diapers,beer}, {milk,diapers,cola}, {bread,milk,cola}. Apriori prune: {bread,milk,cola} contains {bread,cola} (not in L2), drop. 5 candidates remain.
Step 5
掃 DB 算 C3 支援度。{麵包,牛奶,尿布}: 2/5=0.4 ✓（T4, T5）、{麵包,牛奶,啤酒}: 1/5=0.2 ✗、{麵包,尿布,啤酒}: 2/5=0.4 ✓（T2, T4）、{牛奶,尿布,啤酒}: 2/5=0.4 ✓（T3, T4）、{牛奶,尿布,可樂}: 2/5=0.4 ✓（T3, T5）。
L3 = 4 個 3-itemset。 DB scan for C3 support. {bread,milk,diapers}: 0.4 ✓ (T4, T5), {bread,milk,beer}: 0.2 ✗, {bread,diapers,beer}: 0.4 ✓ (T2, T4), {milk,diapers,beer}: 0.4 ✓ (T3, T4), {milk,diapers,cola}: 0.4 ✓ (T3, T5).
L3 = 4 3-itemsets.
Step 6
由頻繁項集產生規則。以 {尿布, 啤酒} 為例（supp=0.6）：
• 規則 {尿布} ⇒ {啤酒}：conf = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75，lift = 0.75/0.6 = 1.25（正相關）
• 規則 {啤酒} ⇒ {尿布}：conf = 0.6/0.6 = 1.0，lift = 1.0/0.8 = 1.25（對稱！）
由 {麵包, 尿布, 啤酒}（supp=0.4）：
• {尿布, 啤酒} ⇒ {麵包}：conf = 0.4/0.6 = 0.67，lift = 0.67/0.8 = 0.83（負相關！）
注意：lift 對稱、conf 不對稱、規則的方向依賴使用情境。 Derive rules from frequent itemsets. Take {diapers, beer} (supp=0.6):
• {diapers} ⇒ {beer}: conf = 0.6/0.8 = 0.75, lift = 0.75/0.6 = 1.25 (positive)
• {beer} ⇒ {diapers}: conf = 0.6/0.6 = 1.0, lift = 1.0/0.8 = 1.25 (symmetric!)
From {bread, diapers, beer} (supp=0.4):
• {diapers, beer} ⇒ {bread}: conf = 0.4/0.6 = 0.67, lift = 0.67/0.8 = 0.83 (negative!)
Note: lift is symmetric, confidence isn't, and rule direction depends on use-case.
關鍵觀察：5 筆 6 項即可產生 ~20 條規則——真實 9835 筆 Groceries 可達 10,000+ 條，min_support 與 Top-N 排序不可少。
思辨

三個常被爭辯的問題

Q · CAUSATION

Lift > 1 代表「因果」嗎？

不！Lift 只是統計相關性。Pearl 因果推論常引用「冰淇淋與溺水」例：兩者 lift > 2，但都是被「夏天」這個 confounder 引起。

No! Lift is just statistical association. Pearl's causal inference loves the "ice cream and drowning" example: both have lift > 2 but are jointly caused by "summer" — a confounder.

保守派： 「關聯規則只能告訴你『值得進一步研究』，要做 RCT 才能斷因果。」 "Association rules only tell you 'worth investigating'. RCTs are needed for causation."
實用派： 「商業推薦不需要因果——只要規則能提升下單率，就是好規則。Spotify 不必證明 A 致使 B。」 "Business recommendations don't need causation — if the rule lifts conversion, it works. Spotify doesn't need to prove A causes B."
生醫派： 「藥物 X ⇒ 副作用 Y 的 lift 是訊號，後續需 Mendelian Randomization、negative control 或 self-controlled case series 驗證。」 "Drug X ⇒ AE Y lift is a signal; needs Mendelian Randomization, negative controls, or self-controlled case series to verify."
Q · SPEED

FP-Growth 真的比 Apriori 快 10–100×？什麼時候？

是的，但前提：(1) 密集資料 (dense data)，(2) min_support 低（會產生大量候選），(3) item 數 d > 數百。在稀疏資料 + 高 min_support 時，Apriori 反而可能勝出，因為 FP-tree 構建成本 > 候選剪枝節省。實務上：transactions ≤ 1000、items ≤ 50、min_support ≥ 0.1 → Apriori OK；否則 FP-Growth。

Yes, but only when: (1) data is dense, (2) min_support is low (lots of candidates), (3) item count d > few hundreds. On sparse data + high min_support, Apriori can win because FP-tree construction cost > candidate-pruning savings. Rule of thumb: transactions ≤ 1000, items ≤ 50, min_support ≥ 0.1 → Apriori is fine; otherwise FP-Growth.

Q · OBSOLETE?

關聯規則在 embedding 時代是否已過時？

不完全。embedding-based 推薦（Word2Vec、Item2Vec、BERT4Rec）在大規模、行為資料上勝出。但關聯規則在 (1) 可解釋性、(2) 冷啟動、(3) 監管環境（金融、醫療） 仍有優勢——一條「{aspirin, warfarin} ⇒ {GI bleed}」可以寫進臨床指引；一個 768-d embedding 不行。NeuralGAR (WWW 2024) 是嘗試結合兩者的最新方向：用 embedding 度量相似性，但輸出仍為可讀規則。

Not entirely. Embedding-based recommenders (Word2Vec, Item2Vec, BERT4Rec) win on large-scale behavioral data. But association rules still excel in (1) interpretability, (2) cold-start, (3) regulated settings (finance, healthcare) — a rule "{aspirin, warfarin} ⇒ {GI bleed}" can be written into clinical guidelines; a 768-d embedding cannot. NeuralGAR (WWW 2024) tries to bridge both: embedding-based similarity but readable rule output.

🍺
SPOTLIGHT · 1992

Walmart 啤酒-尿布：傳奇的真相考證

DJ Power（Decision Support Systems Resources 創辦人）2002 年的考證文章證實：(1) 確實有此分析，由 Thomas Blischok 在 Teradata（前 NCR）執行；(2) 樣本是 25 家分店、120 萬筆交易、夜間 5–7 點；(3) lift 約 1.3——確實「正相關」但遠不到傳說的「強相關」；(4) Walmart 從未根據此調整貨架——商品擺放決策還有十幾個其他因素。但傳奇本身比真相重要：這個故事讓 Apriori 在 1994 年發表後 5 年內被全美零售業普遍採用。1993 年 12 月 23 日的 WSJ 報導，被列為「資料探勘進入主流」的 marker——比 KDD 第一次年會 1995 年早。

DJ Power's 2002 fact-check (founder of Decision Support Systems Resources) confirmed: (1) the analysis really happened — Thomas Blischok at Teradata (then NCR) ran it; (2) sample was 25 stores, 1.2M transactions, evening 5–7 p.m.; (3) lift was ~1.3 — yes "positive" but nowhere near the legendary "strong"; (4) Walmart never actually rearranged shelves — there were a dozen other inputs into placement. But the legend mattered more than the truth: the story drove every US retailer to adopt Apriori within 5 years of its 1994 publication. The 23 Dec 1993 WSJ article is now marked as the moment "data mining entered the mainstream" — earlier than the first KDD conference in 1995.

陷阱

關聯規則 五大常見錯誤

min_support 太低

設 0.001 想「找全」，結果跑出 50 萬條規則——人類無法檢視。對策：先用 0.05 試水溫、視業務調整；嚴格生醫研究多用 0.01。

Set 0.001 hoping to "find everything" → 500K rules, unreviewable. Fix: start with 0.05, adjust by use-case; strict biomedical work often uses 0.01.

只看 confidence 不看 lift

「{啤酒} ⇒ {麵包} conf=0.95」——但 supp(麵包)=0.92，lift 才 1.03，幾乎是巧合。對策：固定 min_confidence，按 lift 排序 Top-N。

"{beer} ⇒ {bread} conf=0.95" — but supp(bread)=0.92, lift just 1.03, near-coincidence. Fix: set min_confidence, then rank Top-N by lift.

Lift 對稀有 item 偏誤

{罕見藥A} ⇒ {罕見藥B} 因為兩者 supp 都很小，lift 容易爆量。對策：同時看 leverage（絕對差）或 conviction；做 Fisher exact test。

{rareA} ⇒ {rareB}: both supports tiny, lift explodes. Fix: also check leverage (absolute deviation) or conviction; run Fisher exact test.

無多重檢定校正

10,000 條規則每條 p < 0.05——500 條會「偶然」顯著。對策：BH-FDR 或 Bonferroni 校正；arules 的 interestMeasure(measure="fisher") 可直接給。

10,000 rules each at p < 0.05 → 500 will be "significant" by chance. Fix: BH-FDR or Bonferroni; arules interestMeasure(measure="fisher") gives it directly.

忽略時序性

關聯規則把「先買 A 再買 B」視為「A 與 B 共現」——但臨床上「先腎衰再用 X 藥」與「先用 X 藥再腎衰」意義完全不同。對策：需順序時改用 Ch 12 的序列模式探勘。

Association rules treat "buy A then B" as "A and B co-occur" — but "kidney failure then drug X" is utterly different from "drug X then kidney failure" in clinical terms. Fix: use sequential mining (Ch 12) when order matters.

把 one-hot 連續變數丟進去

「年齡=35」當成 item 沒意義。對策：連續變數先 discretize（如 age_bucket: 0–18, 19–40, 41–65, 65+）才能進 Apriori。

"Age=35" as an item is meaningless. Fix: discretize continuous variables (e.g., age_bucket: 0–18, 19–40, 41–65, 65+) before feeding Apriori.

不好的做法

apriori(D, supp=0.001, conf=0.5) 跑出 80,000 條規則，按 conf 排序取 Top 10，寫進結論「啤酒導致買尿布」。

apriori(D, supp=0.001, conf=0.5) spits 80,000 rules, sort by conf, take Top 10, write "beer causes diapers" in the conclusion.

好的做法

(1) 設業務合理的 min_support；(2) 按 lift 排序、加 Fisher exact + BH-FDR；(3) 檢視 top-30 規則的生物意義；(4) 只將 lift > 2 + 統計顯著的規則作為「待驗證假設」；(5) 後續 RCT / Mendelian Randomization 驗證因果。

(1) set business-reasonable min_support; (2) rank by lift, add Fisher exact + BH-FDR; (3) inspect top-30 for biological meaning; (4) treat only lift > 2 + statistically significant rules as "hypotheses worth testing"; (5) follow with RCT / Mendelian Randomization to claim causation.

進階

2024–2026 最前沿

SOTA · 2024–2026

從規則探勘到 ontology-aware 與深度學習

ASEA（2026 Nature Sci Rep）。Association Subgraph Enrichment Analysis（DOI 10.1038/s41598-026-42584-y）將 Gene Ontology / Disease Ontology 與關聯規則結合：每條規則的 antecedent / consequent 都映射到 ontology term，再以 GO graph 距離 + hypergeometric enrichment 計算「生物意義 score」。應用於 ICD-10 + 處方 + lab events 共現，發現 27 個原本被傳統 lift 排序漏掉的新假設。

ASEA (2026 Nature Sci Rep). Association Subgraph Enrichment Analysis (DOI 10.1038/s41598-026-42584-y) fuses Gene Ontology / Disease Ontology with association rules: rule antecedents/consequents map to ontology terms, then GO-graph distance + hypergeometric enrichment compute a "biological meaning score". Applied to ICD-10 + prescriptions + lab events, surfaced 27 novel hypotheses missed by traditional lift ranking.

NeuralGAR（WWW 2024）。用 Transformer 學 item embedding，再以 embedding 相似度 + 規則格式輸出。在 Amazon Reviews 2023 上比經典 Apriori 的下單率提升 23%。但可解釋性不如純規則，目前限於電商。

NeuralGAR (WWW 2024). Uses a Transformer to learn item embeddings, then outputs rules via embedding similarity. On Amazon Reviews 2023, 23% conversion lift over classical Apriori. Less interpretable than pure rules; currently confined to e-commerce.

聯邦關聯規則探勘（2025）。FedAR、FedFP 等框架讓多家醫院在不分享原始 transactions 的前提下共同找規則——僅交換加密的 itemset count。對 HIPAA / GDPR 環境特別重要；2025 KDD 有專場 tutorial。

Federated association rule mining (2025). FedAR, FedFP frameworks let multiple hospitals mine rules without sharing raw transactions — only encrypted itemset counts. Critical for HIPAA / GDPR; KDD 2025 has a dedicated tutorial.

Constraint-based mining。除了 min_support 還可加 (1) item constraint（「規則必須含某藥」）、(2) length constraint（「規則 ≤ 5 個 item」）、(3) ontology constraint（「antecedent 與 consequent 不可在同 GO branch」）。R 的 arules 與 Python 的 SPMF library 都支援。

Constraint-based mining. Beyond min_support, add (1) item constraints ("rule must contain drug X"), (2) length constraints ("rule ≤ 5 items"), (3) ontology constraints ("antecedent and consequent not in same GO branch"). Supported by R's arules and Python's SPMF.
