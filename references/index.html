補充說明 · Notes

📌 Ch.11 ASEA 引用

Ch.11 中提到的 ASEA 演算法引自 Scientific Reports 2026 論文（DOI 10.1038/s41598-026-42584-y）。在不同的 itemset/Eclat 評估資料集中其相對效能可能不同；建議讀者在採用前先以自身資料 benchmark。

The ASEA algorithm referenced in Ch.11 comes from Scientific Reports 2026 (DOI 10.1038/s41598-026-42584-y). Its relative performance vs Apriori / FP-Growth / Eclat may vary by dataset; benchmark on your own data before adopting.

補充說明 · Notes

📌 Ch.13 PyOD 版本

教程示例使用 PyOD 2.x API；自 PyOD 3 起 API 採三層架構但保持 fit/predict 向下相容。注意 LLM-based model selection 仍為 PyOD 2 才有的 highlight 功能。

Tutorial uses PyOD 2.x API; from PyOD 3 onward the API is restructured into 3 layers but preserves fit/predict backward compatibility. The LLM-powered model selection is a PyOD 2 highlight.

補充說明 · Notes

📌 Ch.20 EU AI Act 時程

EU AI Act 條文 2024 年生效，但分階段強制執行：(a) 通用 AI 模型 (GPAI) 義務於 2026-08-02 啟動；(b) 高風險系統 (含醫療裝置 AI) 義務於 2027-08-02 啟動。教程內提到的時程依此區分。

The EU AI Act entered into force in 2024 but is enforced in phases: (a) GPAI obligations from 2026-08-02; (b) high-risk system obligations (including AI medical devices) from 2027-08-02.

補充說明 · Notes

📌 Ch.17 OMOP CDM 版本

課程示例採 OMOP CDM v5.4 (2024) 結構欄位（DRUG_EXPOSURE 等）。未來 v6 規格仍在 OHDSI WG 評估中，部分欄位可能會調整；請以官方 spec 為準。

📌 Ch.11 Apriori vs FP-Growth 記憶體權衡 (Han 2000)

教程列舉關聯規則挖掘演算法時未充分區分 Apriori (Agrawal & Srikant 1994) 與 FP-Growth (Han, Pei & Yin 2000 SIGMOD) 的核心差異：(a) Apriori 採 BFS + 多次資料庫掃描（每個 k 階候選需掃一次），低記憶體但高 I/O，適合超出 RAM 的超大型資料集；(b) FP-Growth 建構 FP-tree 後僅需兩次掃描，低 I/O 但高 RAM（FP-tree 在最壞情況可能比原始資料更大）；(c) 當 minsup 低或 item 數爆炸時，FP-tree 在密集資料上反而會比 Apriori 更慢（樹擴張過大）。實務建議：稀疏交易資料 (retail basket) → FP-Growth；密集二元矩陣 (基因表達二元化) → Eclat 或 LCM；超大磁碟駐留資料 → Apriori 仍有價值。mlxtend 與 SPMF 提供三者實作可直接 benchmark。

The tutorial under-distinguishes Apriori (Agrawal & Srikant 1994) and FP-Growth (Han, Pei & Yin 2000 SIGMOD): (a) Apriori uses BFS with multiple DB scans (one per k-itemset level) — low memory, high I/O, suitable for datasets exceeding RAM; (b) FP-Growth builds an FP-tree after just two scans — low I/O, high RAM (FP-tree worst-case can exceed source data size); (c) at low minsup or with item explosion, FP-tree on dense data can be slower than Apriori due to tree blowup. Guidance: sparse transactions (retail baskets) → FP-Growth; dense binary matrices (binarized gene expression) → Eclat or LCM; very large disk-resident data → Apriori is still useful. mlxtend and SPMF provide all three for direct benchmarking.

📌 Ch.13 Outlier ≠ Anomaly 區別 (Chandola 2009)

教程在離群值偵測章節未嚴格區分 Outlier 與 Anomaly，常被學習者混用。Chandola, Banerjee & Kumar 2009 (ACM Computing Surveys) 標準綜述定義：(a) Outlier 是統計性概念，指相對於資料分佈在某距離/密度度量下偏遠的點（如 IQR×1.5 規則、Z-score > 3），不必然具語意異常性；(b) Anomaly 是領域語意層概念，指違反「正常行為定義」的觀察（如信用卡詐欺、ICU 病情惡化），不一定是統計上的離群點。實務影響：(a) 高斯分佈尾端的「outlier」可能完全正常（如身高 195cm 的人）；(b) 真正的 anomaly 可能藏在分佈中央（如偽冒交易模仿正常消費模式）。Chandola 進一步分類 anomaly 為 point / contextual / collective 三型；point anomaly detection 是最簡單情境，contextual (時序、空間) 需建模 context attribute，collective 需建模 sequence/group pattern。

The tutorial does not strictly distinguish outlier from anomaly and learners conflate them. Chandola, Banerjee & Kumar 2009 (ACM Computing Surveys) canonical survey defines: (a) outlier is a statistical concept — a point distant under some distance/density metric (IQR×1.5, Z > 3), not necessarily semantically anomalous; (b) anomaly is a domain-semantic concept — observation violating "normal behavior definition" (credit card fraud, ICU deterioration), not necessarily a statistical outlier. Practical impact: (a) tail "outliers" of a Gaussian may be perfectly normal (195cm person); (b) real anomalies may hide in the distribution center (fraud mimicking normal patterns). Chandola further classifies anomaly into point / contextual / collective — point anomaly is the easiest setting; contextual (temporal, spatial) requires context attributes; collective requires sequence/group pattern modeling.

📌 Ch.9 k-means K 選擇：elbow / silhouette / gap

教程示範 k-means 時主要依 elbow method (within-cluster SS 對 k 圖) 視覺選 K，但 elbow 在實務上常無明確「肘點」。建議補充三大方法：(a) Silhouette coefficient (Rousseeuw 1987, J. Computational and Applied Mathematics)：每點計算 s = (b−a)/max(a,b)，全資料平均 ∈[−1,1]，越接近 1 越好；可同時診斷個別點分群品質。(b) Gap statistic (Tibshirani, Walther & Hastie 2001, JRSS-B)：將實際 within-cluster SS 與從均勻分佈生成的參考資料比較，取 Gap(k) 最大且 Gap(k) ≥ Gap(k+1)−s_{k+1} 的 k；理論基礎較強。(c) BIC / AIC (GMM 適用) 與 (d) consensus clustering（穩定性）。實務上建議 silhouette + gap 雙重檢核；單看 elbow 容易高估或低估 K。NbClust (R) 與 yellowbrick (Python) 可自動跑 20+ 指標投票。

The k-means demos rely on elbow method (within-cluster SS vs k) for visual K selection, but elbows are often ambiguous in practice. Supplement with: (a) Silhouette coefficient (Rousseeuw 1987, J. Computational and Applied Mathematics): per-point s = (b−a)/max(a,b), dataset mean ∈[−1,1] — closer to 1 is better; also diagnoses per-point cluster fit. (b) Gap statistic (Tibshirani, Walther & Hastie 2001, JRSS-B): compares actual within-cluster SS to that under a uniform reference distribution; pick smallest k where Gap(k) ≥ Gap(k+1)−s_{k+1} — stronger theoretical grounding. (c) BIC / AIC (for GMM) and (d) consensus clustering (stability). Practical advice: cross-check silhouette + gap; elbow alone over- or under-estimates K. NbClust (R) and yellowbrick (Python) run 20+ indices in a vote.

📌 Ch.10 DBSCAN 參數敏感性 vs HDBSCAN (Campello 2013)

教程介紹 DBSCAN (Ester et al. 1996) 時建議以 k-distance plot 選 ε，但實務上：(a) ε 對結果極度敏感，差 5% 可能讓整個 cluster 結構崩塌；(b) DBSCAN 假設全域密度均勻，當資料有不同密度的群（多 manifold 混合）時無法同時挑出疏密群；(c) 高維下 ε 選擇受維度詛咒影響極大。Campello, Moulavi & Sander 2013 (PAKDD) 提出 HDBSCAN 解決方案：(a) 將 DBSCAN 從單一 ε 推廣為跨所有 ε 的階層密度估計；(b) 只需設定 min_cluster_size（語意比 ε 直觀）；(c) 自動偵測不同密度的群並輸出穩定性分數；(d) 對雜訊點自動賦予「軟」群成員機率 (Campello 2015 ACM TKDD)。McInnes 等 (2017, J. Open Source Software) 的 hdbscan Python 套件已是 scRNA-seq、UMAP 後續分群的事實標準。建議：教學示範時並列 DBSCAN 與 HDBSCAN 並展示密度不均的反例。

When introducing DBSCAN (Ester et al. 1996), the tutorial recommends k-distance plot for ε selection, but in practice: (a) ε is extremely sensitive — 5% change can collapse cluster structure; (b) DBSCAN assumes globally uniform density and cannot capture clusters of different densities simultaneously (multi-manifold mixtures); (c) ε selection suffers severely from curse of dimensionality. Campello, Moulavi & Sander 2013 (PAKDD) proposed HDBSCAN: (a) generalizes DBSCAN from single ε to a hierarchical density estimate across all ε; (b) only requires min_cluster_size (more semantically intuitive than ε); (c) auto-detects clusters of varying density with stability scores; (d) provides "soft" cluster membership probability for noise (Campello 2015 ACM TKDD). The hdbscan Python package (McInnes et al. 2017, JOSS) is now de facto standard for scRNA-seq and post-UMAP clustering. Recommendation: present DBSCAN and HDBSCAN side-by-side with a varying-density counterexample.

📌 Ch.11 關聯規則 support / confidence / lift 解讀

教程列出 support、confidence、lift 三大指標但未充分提醒其各自的誤用陷阱：(a) Support 是無條件機率 P(A∩B)，過低（<0.001）的規則統計上不可靠，過高則發現「人盡皆知」規則；(b) Confidence 是條件機率 P(B|A)，但會被 B 的基線盛行率欺騙：若 P(B)=0.9，幾乎任何 A 的 confidence 都會 ≥0.9，看似強規則實則無資訊；(c) Lift = P(B|A)/P(B) 修正了 (b)，lift > 1 表示正關聯，lift = 1 為獨立，lift < 1 為負關聯；但 lift 對稀有 itemset 不穩定（變異大）。建議補充 (d) Conviction = (1−P(B))/(1−conf) 對方向性敏感、(e) Leverage = P(A∩B)−P(A)P(B) 量化絕對偏離、(f) Brin et al. 1997 SIGMOD 提出的 χ² 與 (g) Tan, Kumar & Srivastava 2004 (Information Systems) 系統比較 21 種對稱/不對稱指標。實務原則：僅靠 confidence 篩選會誤導；至少加上 lift 過濾，並對稀疏項目用置信區間或 minimum-occurrence 門檻。

The tutorial lists support / confidence / lift but under-warns their pitfalls: (a) Support is the unconditional P(A∩B); too low (<0.001) is statistically unreliable, too high surfaces trivial rules; (b) Confidence is conditional P(B|A) but can be fooled by B's baseline prevalence: if P(B)=0.9, almost any A yields confidence ≥0.9 — looks strong, conveys no information; (c) Lift = P(B|A)/P(B) corrects (b), with lift > 1 positive, = 1 independent, < 1 negative — but is unstable on rare itemsets (high variance). Add (d) Conviction = (1−P(B))/(1−conf) — direction-sensitive; (e) Leverage = P(A∩B)−P(A)P(B) — absolute deviation; (f) χ² from Brin et al. 1997 SIGMOD; (g) Tan, Kumar & Srivastava 2004 (Information Systems) systematic comparison of 21 symmetric/asymmetric measures. Rule: confidence alone misleads; always add lift, and apply confidence intervals or minimum-occurrence thresholds for rare items.
