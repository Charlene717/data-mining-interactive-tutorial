故事化導讀

🌙 凌晨三點，AUROC = 0.99 的時刻

那是 2024 年深秋的醫學中心。資料科學家 Maya 正在訓練一個 30 天再入院預測模型——這是醫院連續第二季的核心 KPI。前一週每晚她都熬到凌晨，從 EHR 撈出 67 萬筆住院紀錄、跑了 14 種演算法、調了三組超參數。

那一晚，AUROC 終於跳到 0.99。她差點要把訊息丟到 Slack 慶祝——但身體卻僵住了。XGBoost 模型不可能在這種資料上拿到 0.99。她重新檢視特徵清單，第 47 個欄位赫然寫著 discharge_diagnosis_icd10——出院時才會填寫的診斷碼。她把它當「入院特徵」餵進了模型。

她默默刪掉那一欄，重跑——AUROC 掉到 0.74。那一刻她才真正理解，為什麼 CRISP-DM 把業務理解與資料理解放在最前面：演算法從來不是專案的瓶頸，對問題與資料的清晰度才是。

It's late autumn 2024 at a major medical center. Data scientist Maya is training a 30-day readmission model — the hospital's KPI for two quarters running. For a week she's stayed until 3 a.m., pulling 670,000 admissions from the EHR, running 14 algorithms, tuning three hyperparameter grids.

Tonight, AUROC jumped to 0.99. She almost hit Slack to celebrate — then froze. XGBoost cannot reach 0.99 on this data. She re-opened the feature list. Column 47: discharge_diagnosis_icd10 — the diagnosis assigned at discharge. She'd fed it as an admission feature.

She quietly removed the column, re-ran — AUROC dropped to 0.74. That was the moment she truly understood why CRISP-DM places Business Understanding and Data Understanding first: the algorithm is never the project bottleneck — clarity about the question and the data is.

Data Mining
資料探勘
想像你在挖恐龍化石——你不知道會挖到什麼，但你知道土裡有故事。
Like digging for dinosaur bones — you don't know what you'll find, but you know the ground holds stories.
從大量資料中萃取非顯而易見、潛在有用、可解釋的模式與知識；位於統計、ML、資料庫與領域知識的交集。
Extracting non-obvious, potentially useful, interpretable patterns and knowledge from large datasets; at the intersection of statistics, ML, databases, and domain expertise.
KDD
知識發現流程
是「挖寶」的全套地圖：先選土壤、再過篩、再分類、最後解釋寶物。
A full treasure-hunting map: pick the soil, sift, sort, then explain what you found.
Fayyad et al. 1996 提出的五階段流程：選擇 → 前處理 → 變形 → 探勘 → 解釋。資料探勘是其中一個步驟。
Fayyad et al. 1996's five-phase process: Selection → Preprocessing → Transformation → Mining → Interpretation. Data mining is one phase within it.
CRISP-DM
跨產業資料探勘標準流程
像食譜書：六個步驟教你怎麼從「我想做什麼」變成「能上桌的成品」。
A cookbook — six steps to take you from "what do I want?" to "ready to serve."
1996 由 SPSS、Daimler-Benz、NCR 提出的六階段循環流程：業務理解 → 資料理解 → 資料準備 → 建模 → 評估 → 部署。
A six-phase cyclic process proposed in 1996 by SPSS, Daimler-Benz, and NCR: Business → Data → Prep → Model → Eval → Deploy.
Methodology
方法論
不是「答案」，而是「找答案的順序」。
Not the answer — it's the order in which you look for the answer.
解決問題的結構化路徑與原則集合；決定團隊如何分工、如何對齊、如何驗證。
A structured path and set of principles for solving problems; determines team workflow, alignment, and validation.
Business Understanding
業務理解
在開挖之前，先問：「我們挖到寶物會拿來幹嘛？值多少錢？」
Before digging: "If we find treasure, what will we do with it? What's it worth?"
界定問題、目標、KPI、限制與成功標準；產出 project charter 與 success metric。
Frame the problem, goals, KPIs, constraints, and success criteria; output: project charter and success metric.
Deployment
部署
把實驗室裡的料理端到客人桌上，還要確保下個月一樣好吃。
Carrying the dish from the lab kitchen to the customer's table — and ensuring it tastes the same next month.
將模型整合至生產環境，含 API 化、文件、監控、再訓練 schedule；常被輕忽。
Integrating the model into production: API exposure, documentation, monitoring, retraining schedule. Often under-invested.
MLOps
機器學習維運
DevOps 的 ML 版：自動上線、自動監控、自動補強。
DevOps for ML: auto-deploy, auto-monitor, auto-retrain.
將 ML 系統的持續整合 / 部署 / 訓練 (CI/CD/CT) 標準化，工具如 MLflow、Vetiver、Kubeflow、ZenML。
Standardizes CI/CD/CT for ML systems — tools: MLflow, Vetiver, Kubeflow, ZenML.
Drift
資料漂移
你訓練時是夏天，部署後變冬天——人類的行為也悄悄變了。
You trained in summer; deployed in winter — human behavior quietly shifted.
輸入分佈 (covariate shift)、標籤分佈 (prior shift)、或 P(y|x) (concept drift) 隨時間改變。需 KS test / PSI / ADWIN 偵測。
Change over time in inputs (covariate), labels (prior), or P(y|x) (concept drift). Detected by KS test / PSI / ADWIN.
Target Leakage
目標外洩
考試前偷看答案——模型「作弊」了。
Peeking at the answer before the exam — the model is cheating.
特徵包含「在預測時間點不該知道的未來資訊」，導致驗證表現虛假升高。
A feature contains "future" information unavailable at prediction time, falsely inflating validation performance.
EDA
探索性資料分析
就像第一次走進新房子——先到處看看，再決定要改裝哪裡。
Like entering a new house — wander around first, then decide what to renovate.
Tukey 1977 提出的視覺化導向資料偵察，先看再測；產出單變量分佈、雙變量關係、品質報表。
Tukey 1977's visualization-first data reconnaissance — look before testing. Outputs: univariate distributions, bivariate plots, quality reports.
Model Governance
模型治理
為每個 AI 訂規矩——誰能改、誰能用、發生錯誤誰要負責。
Setting house rules for AI — who can change it, who can use it, who's accountable.
含 model cards、audit log、approval workflow、rollback 機制；金融與醫療法規常強制 (EU AI Act 2026)。
Includes model cards, audit logs, approval workflows, rollback. Mandated in finance/health regulation (EU AI Act 2026).
Pipeline
資料管線
像一條洗車道，車進去 → 洗 → 蠟 → 吹乾 → 出來。資料也照順序走。
A carwash track: car in → wash → wax → dry → out. Data flows the same way.
將前處理 + 模型串成一個可重現的 DAG；sklearn Pipeline、tidymodels workflow、Snakemake、Airflow。
Chains preprocessing + model into a reproducible DAG — sklearn Pipeline, tidymodels workflow, Snakemake, Airflow.

任務分類

一、資料探勘的六大核心任務

資料探勘 (Data Mining) 是從大量資料中挖掘出非顯而易見、潛在有用、可解釋的模式 (patterns) 與知識 (knowledge) 的過程。它座落於統計學、機器學習、資料庫與領域知識的交集，是 KDD (Knowledge Discovery in Databases) 流程中的核心步驟。

在生物醫學脈絡下，資料探勘的對象橫跨 omics 矩陣、EHR 結構化欄位、影像、PubMed 文獻、臨床路徑等異質資料，常見任務包含：分類疾病 vs 健康、分群病人亞型、發現基因—疾病關聯、預測再入院風險。

Data Mining is the process of extracting non-obvious, potentially useful, interpretable patterns and knowledge from large datasets. It sits at the intersection of statistics, machine learning, databases, and domain expertise — and is the core step inside KDD (Knowledge Discovery in Databases).

In a biomedical context, data mining targets omics matrices, structured EHR fields, images, PubMed literature, and clinical pathways. Typical tasks include disease vs healthy classification, patient subgroup clustering, gene–disease association discovery, and readmission risk prediction.

💡
核心原則：資料探勘不是「跑模型就好」。每一個成功的專案——尤其是醫療領域——必定包含明確的業務問題、嚴謹的資料理解、與審慎的部署評估。方法論（不是演算法）才是專案成敗的關鍵。 Core principle: Data mining is never "just run a model." Every successful project — especially in healthcare — must contain a well-framed business question, rigorous data understanding, and careful deployment evaluation. Methodology, not the algorithm, decides project success.
任務分類

一、資料探勘的六大核心任務

🎯

分類 Classification

給定已標記資料，學習把新樣本歸入離散類別。例：判斷腫瘤良/惡性。代表方法 Logistic、Tree、SVM、NN。

Learn from labeled data to assign new samples into discrete classes. e.g. benign vs malignant tumor. Methods: Logistic, Tree, SVM, NN.

📈

迴歸 Regression

預測連續數值。例：估算病患住院天數、血糖值。代表方法線性、Ridge、隨機森林迴歸、XGBoost。

Predict a continuous numeric value. e.g. length of stay, blood glucose. Methods: linear, Ridge, random forest regression, XGBoost.

🧩

分群 Clustering

非監督學習：發現資料中的自然群組。例：病人亞型發現。代表方法 K-Means、Hierarchical、DBSCAN、GMM。

Unsupervised: find natural groups in data. e.g. patient subtype discovery. Methods: K-Means, Hierarchical, DBSCAN, GMM.

🛒

關聯規則

從交易/共現中找「A → B」規則。例：購物籃分析、藥物—副作用共現、症狀群組。Apriori、FP-Growth、Eclat。

Find "A → B" rules from transactional/co-occurrence data. e.g. market basket, drug–adverse event co-occurrence. Apriori, FP-Growth, Eclat.

🚨

異常偵測 Anomaly

標識偏離常態的觀測值。例：信用卡詐欺、ICU 生理異常、實驗批次效應。Isolation Forest、LOF、ECOD、Autoencoder。

Identify observations that deviate from the norm. e.g. credit-card fraud, ICU physiologic anomaly, batch effects. Isolation Forest, LOF, ECOD, Autoencoder.

🔗

序列樣式 Sequence

從有時間或順序的資料中挖掘共同模式。例：臨床用藥路徑、網站點擊流。GSP、SPADE、PrefixSpan。

Find common patterns from time- or order-ordered data. e.g. clinical prescription pathways, clickstreams. GSP, SPADE, PrefixSpan.

⛏️
生活比喻

資料探勃就像考古學

考古學家不會在沒地圖、沒問題意識下隨便挖掘。他們先研究文獻、選定遺址（業務理解），再小心剖面（資料理解），用刷子層層清理（資料準備），最後拼接器物（建模）並把故事寫進博物館展板（部署）。資料探勘是「在資料土壤裡挖故事」——你不會知道每次挖出的是恐龍化石還是塑膠玩具，但你必定要先確定挖的是值得的地點。

Archaeologists don't dig randomly without a map or research question. They study literature, select a site (Business Understanding), carefully excavate cross-sections (Data Understanding), brush layer by layer (Data Preparation), assemble artifacts (Modeling), and write the story onto museum panels (Deployment). Data mining is "excavating stories from data soil" — you can't predict every dig will surface dinosaur bones or plastic toys, but you must first confirm the dig site is worthwhile.

CRISP-DM

二、CRISP-DM 六階段流程

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) 由 1996 年 SPSS、Daimler-Benz、NCR 共同提出，至 2025 年仍是業界最廣泛使用的方法論。即使後續出現 KDD、SEMMA、TDSP、bizML、CRISP-ML(Q) 等變體，多數仍保留它的六階段骨架——只是擴充了 MLOps / 模型治理 / AI 倫理等步驟。

CRISP-DM (Cross-Industry Standard Process for Data Mining) was proposed in 1996 by SPSS, Daimler-Benz, and NCR. As of 2025 it remains the most widely adopted methodology in industry. Even newer variants — KDD, SEMMA, TDSP, bizML, CRISP-ML(Q) — preserve its six-phase skeleton and only extend it with MLOps / model governance / AI ethics steps.

業務理解

釐清目標與限制：你到底要回答什麼問題？成功標準是什麼？例：「為 2026 年 Q3 上線的 30 天再入院預測模型定義 AUROC ≥ 0.78 並可解釋。」

Clarify goal and constraints: what question are you answering? Success criteria? e.g. "AUROC ≥ 0.78, interpretable, deployed Q3 2026 for 30-day readmission."

資料理解

收集、描述、探索、驗證資料品質。常見產出：data dictionary、缺失率報表、單變量分佈圖。

Collect, describe, explore, and verify data quality. Common outputs: data dictionary, missingness report, univariate distribution plots.

資料準備

清理、整合、變形、特徵工程。實務上佔總時間 60–80%。產出：可分析的 analytic dataset。

Cleaning, integration, transformation, feature engineering. In practice 60–80% of total time. Output: ready-to-model analytic dataset.

模型建構

選擇與訓練模型、調參、做 cross-validation。注意：常需回到第 3 步補做特徵工程。

Select and train models, tune hyperparameters, run cross-validation. Note: you frequently loop back to Step 3 for more feature engineering.

評估

不只看 AUROC——還要評估業務影響、fairness、可解釋性。是否真能回答 Step 1 的問題？

Don't just look at AUROC — also evaluate business impact, fairness, interpretability. Does it actually answer the Step 1 question?

部署

整合到正式系統、文件化、監控漂移 (drift)、定期再訓練。這是 CRISP-DM 最常被輕忽的階段。

Integrate into production, document, monitor drift, schedule retraining. This is the most under-invested phase of CRISP-DM.

⚠️
CRISP-DM 的特色是「循環」而非「線性」：箭頭從 Evaluation 可以回到 Business Understanding；從 Modeling 可以回到 Data Preparation。實務上一個 EHR 探勘專案往往循環 3–5 次。 CRISP-DM is "cyclic," not linear: arrows can flow from Evaluation back to Business Understanding, and from Modeling back to Data Preparation. A real-world EHR mining project typically iterates 3–5 times.

🌳 該選哪個方法論？

Q1:
學術或概念性練習？→ 是 → 使用 KDD（含 Data Selection / Preprocessing / Transformation / Mining / Interpretation）。
Q2:
實務專案、需向業務說明？→ 是 → 用 CRISP-DM；六階段語言業界都熟。
Q3:
專案最終要部署 MLOps？→ 是 → 用 CRISP-ML(Q) 或 TDSP，補上 QA / Monitoring / Retraining 環節。
Q4:
專案要回答業務 KPI 而非演算法精度？→ 是 → 採 bizML，用「業務語言」對齊團隊。
Q1:
Academic or conceptual exercise? → Yes → Use KDD (Data Selection / Preprocessing / Transformation / Mining / Interpretation).
Q2:
Real project, must explain to business? → Yes → Use CRISP-DM — the six-phase vocabulary is universal in industry.
Q3:
Project ends in production MLOps? → Yes → Use CRISP-ML(Q) or TDSP for QA / Monitoring / Retraining.
Q4:
Project must answer business KPI, not algorithmic accuracy? → Yes → Use bizML for business-language alignment.
程式碼

實作骨架：CRISP-DM 在 R / Python

# 1. Business understanding → 寫成註解 / Write as comments
# Goal: predict 30-day hospital readmission. Target AUROC >= 0.78

# 2. Data understanding
library(tidyverse); library(tidymodels); library(skimr)
df <- read_csv("readmission.csv")
skim(df)             # 缺失、分佈、唯一值 / missing, distribution, uniqueness

# 3. Data preparation — recipe-based workflow
rec <- recipe(readmit ~ ., data = df) |>
  step_impute_median(all_numeric_predictors()) |>
  step_dummy(all_nominal_predictors()) |>
  step_normalize(all_numeric_predictors())

# 4. Modeling
mod <- logistic_reg(penalty=tune()) |> set_engine("glmnet")
wf  <- workflow() |> add_recipe(rec) |> add_model(mod)
folds <- vfold_cv(df, v=10, strata=readmit)

# 5. Evaluation
res <- tune_grid(wf, folds, metrics=metric_set(roc_auc, pr_auc))
show_best(res, "roc_auc")

# 6. Deployment — package final model + monitoring hook
library(vetiver)
v <- vetiver_model(fit(finalize_workflow(wf, select_best(res)), df),
                  "readmit_model_v1")
import pandas as pd
from sklearn.model_selection import StratifiedKFold, GridSearchCV
from sklearn.pipeline       import Pipeline
from sklearn.compose        import ColumnTransformer
from sklearn.preprocessing  import StandardScaler, OneHotEncoder
from sklearn.impute         import SimpleImputer
from sklearn.linear_model   import LogisticRegression
from sklearn.metrics        import roc_auc_score

# 1+2 Business + Data understanding
df = pd.read_csv("readmission.csv")
y  = df.pop("readmit")
df.describe(include="all"); df.isna().mean()

# 3. Data preparation (column transformer)
num = df.select_dtypes("number").columns
cat = df.select_dtypes("object").columns
pre = ColumnTransformer([
    ("num", Pipeline([("imp", SimpleImputer(strategy="median")),
                       ("sc",  StandardScaler())]), num),
    ("cat", OneHotEncoder(handle_unknown="ignore"), cat)
])

# 4. Modeling + CV
pipe = Pipeline([("pre", pre), ("clf", LogisticRegression(max_iter=2000))])
cv   = StratifiedKFold(10, shuffle=True, random_state=42)
gs   = GridSearchCV(pipe, {"clf__C": [0.01, 0.1, 1, 10]},
                    scoring="roc_auc", cv=cv, n_jobs=-1)
gs.fit(df, y)
print(gs.best_score_, gs.best_params_)

# 5+6 Eval + deployment skeleton
import joblib, mlflow
joblib.dump(gs.best_estimator_, "readmit_v1.joblib")
mlflow.log_metric("auroc", gs.best_score_)
💡
提醒：在程式碼中為每個 CRISP-DM 階段加上明顯註解（如 # 3. Data Preparation），未來自己或他人接手時可以清楚追蹤流程，這是 reproducibility 的第一步。 Pro tip: Mark each CRISP-DM phase with an explicit comment (e.g. # 3. Data Preparation) in your code. When you or others revisit later, the workflow is traceable — this is reproducibility step #1.
互動：階段時間分配

思辨

三個值得爭論的問題

爭論 #1

CRISP-DM 在 LLM 時代還相關嗎？

當 GPT-5、Claude Opus 4.7 可以「直接讀 CSV + 給出洞見」時，我們還需要六階段方法論嗎？
When GPT-5 or Claude Opus 4.7 can "read a CSV and give insights directly," do we still need a six-phase methodology?
支持方： CRISP-DM 的精華是「問對問題 + 對齊團隊 + 監控部署」——這些跟用什麼模型無關。LLM 只是工具，不是流程。 CRISP-DM's essence is "ask the right question + align the team + monitor deployment" — independent of which model. LLM is a tool, not a process.
反對方： LLM 工作流是新模式：prompt engineering、RAG、agent 編排。六階段命名（建模、調參）跟它對應不上，需要新方法論（如 2025 Anthropic 的 Agent SDLC）。 LLM workflows are a new paradigm — prompt engineering, RAG, agent orchestration. Six-phase naming (modeling, tuning) doesn't map; we need new methodologies (e.g., Anthropic Agent SDLC 2025).
爭論 #2

AI 倫理應該是第 7 階段嗎？

2026 EU AI Act 生效後，許多人主張在六階段後加入「Ethics & Governance」第七階段。
With the EU AI Act in force from 2026, many propose adding a 7th "Ethics & Governance" phase.
支持方： 獨立階段可確保 fairness audit / model card / bias mitigation 不被略過；CRISP-ML(Q) 已經先做。 A separate phase guarantees fairness audits / model cards / bias mitigation are not skipped; CRISP-ML(Q) already moves in this direction.
反對方： 倫理該貫穿所有階段而非孤立步驟——例如業務理解就要問「誰會受影響？」而非把它推到最後。 Ethics should be cross-cutting, not isolated — Business Understanding must already ask "who is affected?" rather than deferring.
爭論 #3

什麼情境 CRISP-DM 會失敗？

CRISP-DM 假設「問題已知 + 資料已存在」。但研究探勘 (research mining) 常常兩者皆無。
CRISP-DM assumes "problem known + data exists." But in research mining, often neither holds.
觀點 A： 在純探索研究中（如 single-cell atlas 探勘），KDD 5 階段更合適——多輪迭代假設、不預設業務 KPI。 In purely exploratory research (e.g., single-cell atlas mining), KDD's 5 phases fit better — iterate hypotheses without a predefined KPI.
觀點 B： CRISP-DM 的「業務理解」其實可以換成「科學問題理解」——只是它的成功標準是「論文可發表」而非「ROI」。 CRISP-DM's "Business Understanding" can be reframed as "Scientific Question Understanding" — the success criterion becomes "publishable paper," not ROI.

經典案例

Netflix Prize 2009：百萬美元的方法論教科書

2006 Netflix 公開 1 億筆評分資料，懸賞 100 萬美元給能將 RMSE 降低 10% 的團隊。三年後，BellKor's Pragmatic Chaos 團隊以 0.8567 → 0.8567 達成（險勝 The Ensemble）。這場比賽完美演繹 CRISP-DM 每一階段：清晰的業務 KPI（RMSE 降低 10%）、嚴謹的資料切割（probe vs qualifying set）、模型 ensemble（>100 個模型 blend）、與最關鍵的未部署結局——Netflix 後來宣佈得獎模型因「engineering complexity」未真正上線，轉而專注於 streaming 推薦。

In 2006, Netflix released 100M ratings and offered $1M to any team that could reduce RMSE by 10%. Three years later, BellKor's Pragmatic Chaos hit 0.8567 (narrowly beating The Ensemble). This contest perfectly illustrated every CRISP-DM phase: a crisp business KPI (10% RMSE reduction), disciplined data splitting (probe vs qualifying set), heavy ensembling (>100 blended models), and — most tellingly — no deployment: Netflix later announced the winning model was never put into production due to "engineering complexity," and they shifted focus to streaming recommendations.

教訓：業務理解（為什麼要做？）與部署可行性（能上線嗎？）比模型本身重要——比賽贏家就栽在這一點。

Lesson: Business Understanding (why?) and Deployment feasibility (can we ship it?) outweigh the model itself — the winners stumbled on exactly this.

CASE STUDY

Google Flu Trends 2008–2015：跳過 CRISP-DM 的代價

2008 年 Google 在 Nature 發表 Flu Trends——用 4,500 萬條搜尋查詢預測流感樣疾病（ILI），自稱「比 CDC 快 1–2 週」。團隊跳過了 CRISP-DM 的 Business Understanding（沒有定義「成功 = 與 CDC 一致」還是「成功 = 預測未來」），也跳過了 Evaluation（從未用 hold-out 季驗證）。2009 H1N1 大流行時模型低估、2012–2013 流感季高估 2 倍以上（Lazer 等 2014 Science）。Google 在 2015 年悄悄關閉服務。事後分析指出三個 CRISP-DM 違規：(1) 把「搜尋頻率」這個會隨 Google 演算法變動的特徵當穩定信號；(2) 沒有 drift 監控；(3) 模型未經過部署→評估→再訓練的循環。

In 2008 Google published Flu Trends in Nature, predicting influenza-like illness (ILI) from 45M search queries and claiming "1–2 weeks ahead of CDC." The team skipped Business Understanding (did "success" mean "agree with CDC" or "predict the future"?) and skipped Evaluation (no held-out season validation). During the 2009 H1N1 pandemic it under-estimated; in 2012–2013 it over-estimated by >2× (Lazer et al. 2014 Science). Google quietly retired the service in 2015. Post-mortem found three CRISP-DM violations: (1) treated "search frequency" — a feature that drifts with Google's own ranking algorithm — as a stable signal; (2) no drift monitoring; (3) no Deployment → Evaluation → Re-train loop.

啟示：沒有 CRISP-DM 的循環，再大的資料、再強的工程團隊也救不了一個沒有 Business Understanding 的模型。

Implication: Without CRISP-DM's loop, no amount of data or engineering talent can rescue a model that skipped Business Understanding.

陷阱

五大常見陷阱

① 跳過業務理解

直接用 .csv 跑 AutoML——三個月後才發現業務方真正想要的是因果推論而非預測。解：強制產出 1 頁 project charter，所有利害關係人簽字。

Jumping straight to AutoML on a .csv — three months later you discover stakeholders wanted causal inference, not prediction. Fix: Require a one-page project charter signed by every stakeholder.

② 無 rollback 計畫

部署當天才發現模型把所有人預測為「高風險」——警示風暴。沒有 A/B 切換能立刻回到舊版本。解：shadow mode + feature flag + canary 5% → 100%。

Deployment day: the model flags everyone as "high risk" — alert storm. No A/B switch to revert. Fix: shadow mode + feature flag + canary 5% → 100%.

③ 無 drift 監控

部署半年後性能默默崩壞——但沒人發現，直到主管問「最近 PPV 怎麼這麼差？」解：PSI / KS / model performance dashboard 自動週報。

Six months in, performance quietly collapsed — no one noticed until the manager asked "why is PPV so low?" Fix: PSI / KS / performance dashboard auto-reported weekly.

④ 忽略利害關係人

資料科學團隊獨立工作，部署時護理師抱怨「按鈕在哪？我已經在 EHR 點 20 下了」——UX 從沒被討論。解：每階段都讓終端使用者參與 review。

DS team works in isolation; at deployment, nurses complain "I've already clicked 20 times in the EHR — where's the button?" UX was never discussed. Fix: end-users review at every phase.

⑤ 只看模型指標

AUROC 0.85 但 PPV 8%——臨床護理師收到 1000 個警示，只有 80 個真的需要關心。模型「準」但臨床上「不可用」。解：用業務指標（NNS、淨效益）取代純 ML 指標。

AUROC 0.85 but PPV 8% — nurses get 1000 alerts, only 80 actionable. The model is "accurate" but clinically unusable. Fix: use business metrics (NNS, net benefit) instead of pure ML metrics.

⑥ 直線思考非循環

把 CRISP-DM 當「跑完六步就結束」——但真實專案需要在 Evaluation → Business Understanding 之間迴圈 3–5 次。解：專案 timeline 必須留 buffer 給「回到上一階段」。

Treating CRISP-DM as "run six steps and finish" — but real projects loop Evaluation → Business Understanding 3–5 times. Fix: project timeline must budget for "return to previous phase."
