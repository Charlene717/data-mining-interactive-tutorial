故事

開場：8,234 篇文獻能在 3 小時讀完嗎？

情境

💊 Dr Lin 的 metformin × 癌症 系統性回顧

藥理學家林教授接到衛福部委託：「請整理 metformin 與所有癌症的關聯，三個月後給臨床建議。」她輸入 PubMed："metformin"[MeSH] AND "Neoplasms"[MeSH]，蹦出 8,234 篇。一篇花 15 分鐘讀，等於 2,058 小時，約一年。她決定改走文獻探勘路線。

她寫了 50 行 Python：Entrez E-utilities 下 ESearch + EFetch 抓 abstract、BERN2 跑 NER 取得 (gene/drug/disease) 實體、PubTator 3.0 API 拿到 36M 篇預標註好的關聯、用 networkx 建知識圖譜、Open Targets API 比對藥物-疾病關聯分數。3 小時跑完，得到 metformin 與 16 種癌症的證據強度排序（colorectal 最強，hepatocellular 中等，pancreatic 證據混雜）。她把每個 claim 都連回原始 PMID，避免 LLM 幻覺。最後她用 BMC Cancer 投稿了這份「半自動 systematic review」，並把 Snakemake workflow 公開。

這就是現代文獻探勘的承諾：不取代閱讀，而是把讀到對的東西的時間壓到原來的 1%。

Pharmacologist Prof. Lin gets a ministry brief: "Synthesize everything on metformin and any cancer in 3 months." She queries PubMed "metformin"[MeSH] AND "Neoplasms"[MeSH] → 8,234 hits. At 15 min/paper that's 2,058 hours — about a year. She switches to literature mining.

50 lines of Python: Entrez E-utilities for ESearch + EFetch, BERN2 NER on (gene / drug / disease), PubTator 3.0 API for 36M pre-annotated abstracts, networkx for a KG, Open Targets API for drug-disease scores. 3 hours later she has metformin vs 16 cancers, ranked by evidence (colorectal strongest, hepatocellular middle, pancreatic mixed). Every claim links back to the original PMID — no LLM hallucinations. She publishes the "semi-automated systematic review" in BMC Cancer, with Snakemake workflow public.

The promise of literature mining is not to replace reading — it is to compress the time to read the right things to 1% of the original.

All medical concepts arranged as a tree: "Drugs → Antineoplastic Agents → Metformin."

NER

電腦讀文章，把「EGFR (基因)」「Tarceva (藥)」「肺癌 (病)」一一標出來。
A computer reads text and labels "EGFR (gene), Tarceva (drug), lung cancer (disease)."
Named Entity Recognition；BERT-based (BioBERT, PubMedBERT) 為 SOTA；F1 在 BC5CDR 0.90+；BERN2 為 multi-type tagger。

關係抽取

Relation Extraction; datasets BC5CDR, CHEMPROT, DDI, GDA; BioRED (Luo 2022) 6,000+ abstracts, 8 relation types; 2024 SOTA GPT-4 in-context F1 ~0.55.

概覽

從關鍵字查詢到知識圖譜

PubMed 由 NLM 維護，2025 年已超過 3,800 萬筆引用。文獻探勘的層次大致為：(1) 檢索 / Boolean，(2) 實體辨識 (NER)，(3) 關係抽取 (RE)，(4) 知識圖譜 (KG) 建構與推理，(5) 藥物再利用 (drug repurposing) 與 假說生成 (Swanson ABC)。

近年 LLM/領域 BERT 大幅提升了 NER 與 RE 的 F1：BioBERT (2019)、PubMedBERT (2020)、SciSpacy、BERN2 (2022)、BioGPT (2022)、Med-Gemini (2024)、BioMistral 7B (2024)。但幻覺 (hallucination) 與引用偽造仍是 LLM 在文獻萃取上的最大風險。

PubMed (NLM) holds 38M+ citations as of 2025. Literature mining layers roughly as: (1) retrieval / Boolean, (2) NER, (3) relation extraction, (4) knowledge-graph construction & inference, (5) drug repurposing and hypothesis generation (Swanson's ABC).

Domain BERTs and LLMs lift NER/RE F1 dramatically: BioBERT (2019), PubMedBERT (2020), SciSpacy, BERN2 (2022), BioGPT (2022), Med-Gemini (2024), BioMistral 7B (2024). But hallucination and fabricated citations remain the biggest LLM risks in literature extraction.

藥物再利用 & KG

三、知識圖譜與藥物再利用

主流生醫 KG：Hetionet (47k node / 2.25M edge, 2017)、BioKG、DRKG (COVID-19, 2020)、PrimeKG (Harvard, 2023/2024 — 4M edges, 17 sources)、OpenTargets Platform、DrugBank 5。藥物再利用核心：在 (drug)–[?]→(disease) 邊上做 link prediction（如 RotatE、TransE、ComplEx、HGNN）。著名案例：sildenafil 從 angina → erectile dysfunction → pulmonary hypertension。

Swanson 的 ABC 假說：若 A→B 與 B→C 都在文獻中，但 A→C 未被連起來，就是可能的新假說（1986 經典 Raynaud × fish oil）。

Mainstream biomedical KGs: Hetionet (47k nodes / 2.25M edges, 2017), BioKG, DRKG (COVID-19, 2020), PrimeKG (Harvard, 2023/2024 — 4M edges, 17 sources), OpenTargets Platform, DrugBank 5. Drug repurposing core: link prediction on (drug)–[?]→(disease) edges (RotatE, TransE, ComplEx, HGNN). Classic: sildenafil from angina → ED → pulmonary hypertension.

Swanson's ABC hypothesis: if A→B and B→C exist in literature but A→C is not yet linked, that is a candidate new hypothesis (1986: Raynaud × fish oil).

實作：E-utilities + SciSpacy NER + KG

library(easyPubMed); library(pubmed.mineR); library(udpipe); library(igraph)

# --- query PubMed via E-utilities ---
q       <- 'metformin[MeSH] AND "Type 2 Diabetes"[MeSH] AND 2024[PDAT]'
ids     <- get_pubmed_ids(q)
abs_xml <- fetch_pubmed_data(ids, retmax = 500)
df      <- articles_to_list(abs_xml) |> articles_to_dataframe()

# --- udpipe biomedical model + tokenize + lemmatize ---
m  <- udpipe::udpipe_download_model(language = "english-ewt")
ud <- udpipe::udpipe_load_model(m$file_model)
tk <- udpipe::udpipe_annotate(ud, x = df$abstract) |> as.data.frame()

# --- co-occurrence at sentence level & KG ---
co <- udpipe::cooccurrence(tk, term = "lemma", group = c("doc_id","sentence_id"))
g  <- igraph::graph_from_data_frame(co)
igraph::plot(g, vertex.size = 5, edge.arrow.size = 0.3)
from Bio import Entrez
import scispacy, spacy
from spacy.lang.en import English
import networkx as nx
Entrez.email = "you@uni.edu"; Entrez.api_key = "…"

# --- E-utilities: ESearch + EFetch ---
h    = Entrez.esearch(db="pubmed", term='metformin[MeSH] AND "Type 2 Diabetes"[MeSH] AND 2024[PDAT]', retmax=500)
ids  = Entrez.read(h)["IdList"]
abs_ = Entrez.efetch(db="pubmed", id=ids, rettype="abstract", retmode="xml").read()

# --- SciSpacy NER (biomed) + linker to UMLS ---
nlp = spacy.load("en_ner_bionlp13cg_md")
from scispacy.linking import EntityLinker
nlp.add_pipe("scispacy_linker", config={"linker_name": "umls", "resolve_abbreviations": True})

# --- entity extraction + sentence co-occurrence KG ---
G = nx.Graph()
for doc in nlp.pipe(abstracts, batch_size=32):
    for sent in doc.sents:
        ents = [(e.text, e.label_) for e in sent.ents]
        for a, b in combinations(ents, 2):
            G.add_edge(a[0], b[0], rel=f"{a[1]}-{b[1]}")

# --- LLM-augmented RE with PubMedBERT ---
from transformers import pipeline
re = pipeline("text-classification", model="microsoft/BiomedNLP-PubMedBERT-base-uncased-abstract-fulltext")
完整實例

Ibuprofen → Alzheimer 藥物再利用 (Step-by-Step)

RP

從 PubMed 查詢到 Open Targets 驗證

假說：NSAIDs 預防 AD
Step 1
PubMed 查詢：Entrez E-utilities ESearch，term = "ibuprofen"[MeSH] AND ("Alzheimer Disease"[MeSH] OR "Cognitive Dysfunction"[MeSH])，加 2015[PDAT]:2024[PDAT] 限時間窗 → 1,247 篇 PMID。記得 ELink 抓 related articles 增加 recall。
PubMed query: Entrez E-utilities ESearch, term "ibuprofen"[MeSH] AND ("Alzheimer Disease"[MeSH] OR "Cognitive Dysfunction"[MeSH]) + 2015[PDAT]:2024[PDAT] → 1,247 PMIDs. Use ELink for related articles to boost recall.
Step 2
批次抓 abstract：用 EPost + WebEnv 把 1247 PMID 分 5 批 EFetch，rate-limit 10/s（有 API key）。XML 解析後得到 title + abstract + MeSH terms + publication type (RCT, observational, review)。
Batch-fetch abstracts: EPost + WebEnv splits 1247 PMIDs into 5 batches of EFetch, rate-limited at 10/s (with API key). XML parse → title + abstract + MeSH + publication type (RCT, observational, review).
Step 3
BERN2 NER：對每篇 title + abstract 跑 BERN2 (HTTP API)，得到 (gene, disease, drug, chemical, species) 標註 + UMLS CUI linking。~12,000 entity mentions，去重後 ~860 unique entities。
BERN2 NER: run BERN2 (HTTP API) on title + abstract → (gene, disease, drug, chemical, species) tags + UMLS CUI linking. ~12,000 entity mentions; ~860 unique after dedup.
Step 4
建 co-occurrence 圖：對每個 sentence 抽出 entity pairs，用 networkx 累加 edge weight。Node = entity，edge weight = co-occurrence count。共 ~3,200 nodes, ~24,000 edges。
Build co-occurrence graph: per sentence, extract entity pairs, accumulate edge weight with networkx. ~3,200 nodes, ~24,000 edges.
Step 5
篩 "ibuprofen × cognitive" pairs：過濾 source/target 至少一個是 NSAID-related (UMLS CUI C0020740, C0007792)、另一個是 cognitive-related (C0002395, C0023895, C0020538) → 87 個候選 pairs。
Filter "ibuprofen × cognitive" pairs: at least one endpoint NSAID-related (UMLS C0020740, C0007792) and the other cognitive-related (C0002395, C0023895, C0020538) → 87 candidate pairs.
Step 6
關係抽取：每個 candidate pair 找回 PMID + sentence context，餵入 BioRED-pretrained PubMedBERT RE 模型；輸出 8 relation labels：positive_correlation, negative_correlation, association, drug_interaction…。聚合每 pair 的 relation distribution。
Relation extraction: retrieve PMID + sentence for each candidate pair, feed to BioRED-pretrained PubMedBERT RE; outputs 8 labels: positive_correlation, negative_correlation, association, drug_interaction…. Aggregate per-pair distribution.
Step 7
排序：每個 pair 計算 support (PMID 數) × lift (P(co)/P(co|null)) × confidence (RE 預測信心)。Top: ibuprofen ↔ COX-2 ↔ Aβ plaque clearance；ibuprofen ↔ microglial activation；ibuprofen ↔ MCI risk。
Rank: each pair scored by support (#PMIDs) × lift (P(co)/P(co|null)) × RE confidence. Top: ibuprofen ↔ COX-2 ↔ Aβ plaque clearance; ibuprofen ↔ microglial activation; ibuprofen ↔ MCI risk.
Step 8
外部驗證：把 top-10 候選送 Open Targets GraphQL API 查 evidence score (ENSG-EFO pair)；4/10 有 ≥ 0.5 整體分數。其中 ibuprofen × Alzheimer 的 Open Targets 證據強度 0.32，主要來自 GWAS (PTGS2 locus) + 文獻；提醒讀者：literature signal ≠ causation，需 RCT 驗證 (見 ADAPT trial 2007 的失敗教訓)。
External validation: top-10 candidates queried via Open Targets GraphQL for evidence score (ENSG-EFO); 4/10 score ≥ 0.5. Ibuprofen × Alzheimer overall score 0.32, mostly GWAS (PTGS2 locus) + literature. Caveat: literature signal ≠ causation; RCTs needed (cf. ADAPT trial 2007 negative result).
🔬 進階：LLM RAG 的引用驗證

OpenEvidence、Glass.health、MedRAG 等臨床 RAG 系統會給 evidence-cited answer，但 2024 Stanford study (Magesh et al.) 發現 1/3 LLM-生成引用為「不存在」或「不支持結論」。對策：(1) 引用必須由檢索器產生 (BM25 / dense)、再由 LLM 引用，不能由 LLM 憑空生成；(2) 跑 citation verifier (Bohnet 2022 attributed QA)；(3) UI 強制顯示每個 claim 的證據句+ PMID。

OpenEvidence, Glass.health, MedRAG produce evidence-cited answers, but Stanford's 2024 audit (Magesh et al.) found 1/3 of LLM-generated citations were non-existent or did not support the claim. Fix: (1) citations must come from a retriever (BM25 / dense), then quoted by the LLM — never invented; (2) run a citation verifier (Bohnet 2022 attributed QA); (3) UI forces showing the evidence sentence + PMID for every claim.

案例

2017 Hetionet — 把 PubMed 變成預測機器

Daniel Himmelstein 與 Casey Greene (2017 eLife) 把 29 個生醫資料庫整合成 Hetionet：47k nodes、2.25M edges、11 種節點類型。他們開發 DWPC (Degree-Weighted Path Count) 演算法找候選 drug repurposing，預測「nicotine 可能預防 Alzheimer」。實驗 (Newhouse 2012) 與後續流行病學研究確實支持這個關聯。其他 top 預測（如 imatinib for asthma）也陸續有實驗 evidence。

Hetionet 的意義是知識圖譜可以推理：A-B-C 串在一起，A → C 就有可能。這成為現代 PrimeKG、DRKG、BioKG 等所有生醫 KG 的藍圖。

Himmelstein & Greene (2017 eLife) merged 29 biomedical sources into Hetionet: 47k nodes, 2.25M edges, 11 node types. Their DWPC (Degree-Weighted Path Count) algorithm predicted candidate drug repurposings — including "nicotine may prevent Alzheimer," which Newhouse 2012 + later epidemiology supported. Other top hits (e.g., imatinib for asthma) also gained experimental backing.

Hetionet's lesson: knowledge graphs can reason. A-B-C means A → C is plausible. This became the blueprint for PrimeKG, DRKG, BioKG, and all modern biomedical KGs.

爭論

三個正在燒的爭論

爭論 1

LLM 會取代專門 NER 模型嗎？

2024–2025 不少論文用 GPT-4 / Claude / Gemini 做 in-context biomedical NER，F1 在某些任務逼近 BERN2、PubMedBERT。但成本、速度、可重現性遠不如專門模型。Tinn et al. 2023 Patterns 證實領域 BERT 在 F1 仍贏 GPT-4 5–10%。
Many 2024–2025 papers use GPT-4 / Claude / Gemini for in-context biomedical NER, approaching BERN2 / PubMedBERT on some tasks. But cost, speed, reproducibility trail far behind specialized models. Tinn et al. 2023 Patterns showed domain BERTs still beat GPT-4 by 5–10% F1.
2026 折衷：大型語料用 BERN2 / PubMedBERT 做主力標註；LLM 用於低資源實體 (rare disease, novel target) 與後驗證 (judge confidence)。
2026 compromise: BERN2 / PubMedBERT for high-volume annotation; LLMs reserved for low-resource entities (rare diseases, novel targets) and post-hoc judging.
爭論 2

開源 vs 封閉醫療 LLM

封閉模型：Med-PaLM 2 (Google)、Med-Gemini (2024 NEJM AI)、GPT-4-Medicine。USMLE 表現超人但模型參數、訓練資料、後處理皆不公開。開源：BioMistral 7B (2024)、Meditron 70B、BioGPT 2022、PMC-LLaMA — 表現次一級但可本地部署+稽核。
Closed: Med-PaLM 2 (Google), Med-Gemini (2024 NEJM AI), GPT-4-Medicine — superhuman USMLE but weights/data/post-processing closed. Open: BioMistral 7B (2024), Meditron 70B, BioGPT 2022, PMC-LLaMA — slightly weaker but locally deployable + auditable.
監管含義：EU AI Act 對 GPAI 要求透明度報告；高風險 AI 還需 risk-management + 技術文件。封閉模型若用於臨床決策，合規難度高。
Regulatory: EU AI Act requires GPAI transparency reports; high-risk AI further needs risk-management + technical docs. Closed models in clinical decision-support face a steep compliance hill.
爭論 3

KG 預測藥物再利用是真發現還是事後印證？

Hetionet, PrimeKG, DRKG 都展示了「成功預測」案例 — 但這些案例往往已經有部分文獻證據。批評者說：KG 只是把已知的關聯重排，沒有真正「新發現」；Wang et al. 2024 Nat Commun 也指出 link prediction benchmark 在真實前瞻性試驗上 hit rate 不到 5%。
Hetionet, PrimeKG, DRKG showcase "successful predictions" — but cases typically had partial prior literature. Critics say KGs reshuffle known associations rather than discover. Wang et al. 2024 Nat Commun show link-prediction benchmarks have a <5% hit rate in true prospective trials.
建設性立場：KG 是 hypothesis-generation 工具，不是發現本身；發現一定要在實驗或臨床試驗中發生。
Constructive view: KGs are hypothesis-generators, not discoveries themselves; discoveries happen only in lab or trial.
陷阱

五個文獻探勘的常見陷阱

未處理同義詞

"acetaminophen" / "paracetamol" / "Tylenol" 都是同一藥物；如果不做 UMLS linking，會把同一概念分成三個 node，KG 全亂。必用 SciSpacy linker 或 PubTator 標準化。

"acetaminophen" / "paracetamol" / "Tylenol" all the same drug. Without UMLS linking, you split one concept into three nodes — KG falls apart. Always use SciSpacy linker or PubTator normalization.

引用偏差

熱門藥物 (metformin, aspirin) 文章數萬篇，冷門新藥可能只有幾十篇。Co-occurrence count 高 ≠ 證據強，要 normalize by paper count 或用 lift / TF-IDF。

Popular drugs (metformin, aspirin) have tens of thousands of papers; novel drugs may have dozens. High co-occurrence ≠ strong evidence — normalize by paper count, or use lift / TF-IDF.

縮寫 false positive

"AD" 可能是 Alzheimer's Disease，也可能是 "average daily"、"adjusted dose"。"CA" 是鈣或加州或 "carcinoma"。必用 scispacy resolve_abbreviations=True + 上下文消歧。

"AD" might be Alzheimer's, "average daily," or "adjusted dose." "CA" is calcium, California, or "carcinoma." Always use scispacy resolve_abbreviations=True + context disambiguation.

MeSH inflation

過度依賴 MeSH explosion 會把無關 sub-terms 全包括進來。對 specific entity 用 [Mesh:NoExp] 或 [tw] (text word) 來精準查詢。

Over-relying on MeSH explosion sweeps in unrelated sub-terms. For specific entities use [Mesh:NoExp] or [tw] (text word) for precise queries.

LLM 偽造引用

2024 Stanford 研究 (Magesh et al.) 發現 1/3 LLM 生成的醫療引用根本不存在或不支持結論。必須用 RAG：先檢索 → 再生成，並對每個 claim 驗證 PMID 與引用句。

Stanford 2024 (Magesh et al.) found 1/3 of LLM-generated medical citations were non-existent or unsupportive. Always use RAG: retrieve first → generate, verify every claim with PMID and the evidence sentence.

把語料偏差當共識

PubMed 偏 Western, English、新藥物多有 industry-sponsored；對發展中國家、非英語、negative results 嚴重不足。文獻探勘的「主流意見」≠ 真實生物事實。

PubMed skews Western, English, industry-sponsored; under-represents developing-country research, non-English, and negative results. "Literature consensus" ≠ biological truth.

Ask GPT-4 to "list 5 papers supporting metformin × pancreatic cancer," paste 5 PMIDs + abstracts into supplementary. 3 PMIDs don't exist, 1 is off-topic, 1 contradicts. Reviewer misses it; reader correspondence exposes after publication.

好範例

Use PubTator 3.0 API for vetted NER+RE annotations, build KG with networkx, every PMID + sentence reproducible in Snakemake + Docker; release GitHub repo + DOI'd dataset.

常見錯誤陷阱 / Common Pitfalls

症狀："NSCLC"、"non-small cell lung cancer"、"非小細胞肺癌" 被當成不同實體；KG 出現 3 個分離節點，下游 evidence count 嚴重低估。修正：用 UMLS Metathesaurus 將所有實體 normalize 到 CUI；scispaCy + UMLSEntityLinker，或 PubTator 3.0 已預作。

Symptom: "NSCLC", "non-small cell lung cancer", and the Chinese term get treated as different entities — three disjoint nodes in the KG, downstream evidence counts undercounted. Fix: normalize all entities to UMLS Metathesaurus CUIs via scispaCy + UMLSEntityLinker, or use PubTator 3.0 (already linked).

症狀：共現分析顯示「aspirin → cancer」連結比「lesser-known drug → cancer」強——但其實只反映 aspirin 被研究得更多。修正：正規化（PMI、Jaccard、TF-IDF over time），或用 Open Targets 整合多源 evidence。

Symptom: co-occurrence analysis shows a stronger "aspirin → cancer" link than "lesser-known drug → cancer" — but it just reflects that aspirin gets studied more. Fix: normalize via PMI, Jaccard, or TF-IDF over time; or use Open Targets to integrate multi-source evidence.

症狀："AD" 既是 Alzheimer's Disease，也是 "average daily" 或 "Anno Domini"；NER 模型若無 abbreviation disambiguation，5–15% 假陽性。修正：用 BioBERT/PubMedBERT 已 fine-tune 的 abbreviation 偵測；或 Schwartz-Hearst 算法找定義；或 PubTator 已處理。

Symptom: "AD" can be Alzheimer's Disease, "average daily" or "Anno Domini"; without abbreviation disambiguation, NER hits 5–15% false positives. Fix: fine-tuned abbreviation detection from BioBERT/PubMedBERT; Schwartz-Hearst algorithm for definitions; or use PubTator (already handled).

症狀：1990 年代 MeSH 標註粒度比 2020 年代細很多——時間序列趨勢分析會看到「假性下降」。修正：計入「文章總數」做 normalization；或限制研究時段。

Symptom: 1990s MeSH was tagged more granularly than 2020s — time-series trend analyses see "phantom declines". Fix: normalize by total article count per year; or restrict the study window.

症狀：ChatGPT / Claude 給出格式正確但不存在的論文（作者 + 期刊 + 年份都對，DOI 是亂造的）。修正：用 retrieval-augmented generation（RAG）；每一個引用都用 NCBI E-utilities API 驗證 PMID；或用 OpenEvidence / Med-Gemini 等 verifiable cite 架構。

Symptom: ChatGPT / Claude returns plausibly formatted but non-existent papers (correct authors + journals + years, but a fabricated DOI). Fix: use retrieval-augmented generation (RAG); verify every citation via NCBI E-utilities by PMID; or use OpenEvidence / Med-Gemini-style verifiable-cite architectures.

症狀：下游 KG 出 5,000 個新邊但實際只有 60% precision；研究者卻只報「edges = 5000」。修正：在 BioRED (Luo 2022) / NCBI Disease / BC5CDR 上算 precision / recall / F1；報告下游 KG 的 evidence threshold。

Symptom: downstream KG yields 5,000 new edges but only 60% precision; the paper still reports "edges = 5000". Fix: compute precision / recall / F1 on BioRED (Luo 2022), NCBI Disease, BC5CDR; report the downstream KG's evidence threshold.

DEBATE

2026 年，我們還需要 BERN2、PubMedBERT、scispaCy 等專用 NER 工具嗎？
In 2026 do we still need specialized NER tools like BERN2, PubMedBERT, scispaCy?
觀點一： 不會取代——LLM 幻覺風險在醫療領域無法接受；PubMedBERT + spaCy 在 BC5CDR / BioRED 上仍是 SOTA。Will not replace — LLM hallucination is unacceptable in medicine; PubMedBERT + spaCy remain SOTA on BC5CDR / BioRED.
觀點二： 會混合——2026 多採用 hybrid 架構：LLM 做 context disambiguation + relation extraction，專用 NER 做 entity normalization。Will hybridize — 2026 mainstream is hybrid: LLM for context disambiguation + relation extraction, specialized NER for entity normalization.
OPEN vs CLOSED

處理臨床文本時，BioMistral / ClinicalBERT 等開源模型，與 GPT-4 Med / Med-Gemini 等私有模型，該選哪一個？
For clinical text, do you pick open-source (BioMistral / ClinicalBERT) or proprietary (GPT-4 Med / Med-Gemini)?
觀點一： 開源——HIPAA 合規偏好 on-premise（資料不出醫院）；2024 BioMistral 7B、OpenLLaMA-Med、ClinicalBERT 適合自託管。Open-source — HIPAA prefers on-premise (data stays in-hospital); 2024 BioMistral 7B, OpenLLaMA-Med, ClinicalBERT suit self-hosting.
觀點二： 私有 (BAA)——研究等級表現仍以 GPT-4 Med、Med-Gemini 領先；走 BAA + Azure HIPAA / Google HealthLake 可合規使用。Proprietary (BAA) — research-grade performance still leads with GPT-4 Med, Med-Gemini; use them HIPAA-compliantly via BAAs (Azure HIPAA, Google HealthLake).
DRUG REPURPOSING

Hetionet、Open Targets 等 KG 預測，到底是在發現新假說，還是只是確認既有直覺？
Are Hetionet / Open Targets predictions discovering novel hypotheses or just confirming intuition?
觀點一： 混合——Hetionet 的 metformin → Alzheimer's 預測在 2017 算新穎；但多數預測仍排到既有 hypothesis 前 5%。Mixed — Hetionet's metformin → Alzheimer prediction was novel in 2017; but most predictions still rank in the top 5% of existing hypotheses.
觀點二： 仍有價值——即使「確認」既有直覺，也能提供量化排序、優先級分配；對 budget-constrained drug discovery 有實用意義。Still valuable — even "confirming" intuition provides quantitative ranking + prioritization; useful for budget-constrained drug discovery.

CASE STUDY

2017 年 Himmelstein 與 Greene 在 eLife 發表 Hetionet：一個整合了 47,031 個節點（化合物、疾病、基因、解剖部位…）與 2,250,197 條邊的知識圖。他們用 DistMult-style embeddings 對邊做 link prediction，並預測「nicotine 可能預防阿茲海默症」。

2018 Newhouse 等在 Neurology 發表臨床試驗 (n=74)：透過 transdermal nicotine 治療輕度認知障礙 (MCI)，顯著改善認知功能。從圖探勘到臨床試驗發表，僅 不到一年。Hetionet 後來成為 KG-based drug repurposing 的範本，影響了今日 Open Targets、PrimeKG、Hetionet 2.0 的設計。所有資料 + 程式碼開放於 GitHub，是「open science + KG mining」的代表作。

In 2017 Himmelstein & Greene published Hetionet in eLife: a knowledge graph integrating 47,031 nodes (compounds, diseases, genes, anatomy, etc.) and 2,250,197 edges. They applied DistMult-style embeddings for link prediction and predicted "nicotine may prevent Alzheimer's".

The 2018 Newhouse trial in Neurology (n=74) showed transdermal nicotine significantly improved cognition in MCI patients. From graph mining to a published clinical trial in under a year. Hetionet became the template for KG-based drug repurposing, shaping Open Targets, PrimeKG, and Hetionet 2.0. All data + code on GitHub — a flagship of "open science + KG mining".

歷史小故事：1879 年 — 第一部系統性的醫學書目《Index Medicus》由 John Shaw Billings 於 U.S. Army Medical Library（後來的 NLM）出版。每一條紀錄都是手抄。Billings 後來還設計了 New York Public Library 的館舍。《Index Medicus》運作了 125 年，2004 年停刊——被 MEDLINE 取代，MEDLINE 又於 1997 年化身為 PubMed。從手寫卡片到 36M+ 數位摘要，只花了 140 年。1986 年 Don Swanson 在 Perspect Biol Med 發表「Fish oil → Raynaud's」假說（A→B→C 連接不同學科文獻），三年後 DiGiacomo RCT 證實——文獻探勘領域自此誕生，今日 PubTator 3.0 + LLM RAG 是 Swanson 思路的工業化版本。1879 — Index Medicus, the first systematic medical bibliography, was published by John Shaw Billings at the U.S. Army Medical Library (now NLM). Every entry was hand-indexed. Billings later designed the New York Public Library's building. Index Medicus ran for 125 years and ceased publication in 2004 — replaced by MEDLINE, which became PubMed in 1997. From handwritten cards to 36M+ digital abstracts in just 140 years. In 1986 Don Swanson's "fish oil → Raynaud's" hypothesis (chaining A→B→C across disjoint literatures) was confirmed three years later by DiGiacomo's RCT — birthing the field of literature mining. PubTator 3.0 + LLM RAG today is the industrialized version of Swanson's hunch.
